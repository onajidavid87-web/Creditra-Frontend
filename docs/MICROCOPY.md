# Microcopy Guide

This document centralizes definitions and style guidelines for financial terms and copy used throughout the Creditra platform. When these terms appear in user interfaces, they should ideally be wrapped in the `InlineTermTooltip` component to provide inline contextual help.

## Core Financial Terms

### APR (Annual Percentage Rate)
- **Definition**: The annual rate charged for borrowing, expressed as a single percentage number that represents the actual yearly cost of funds over the term of a loan.
- **Usage**: Always capitalize as "APR". When displaying rates, include the `%` symbol directly after the number without a space (e.g., `12.5% APR`).

### Credit Limit
- **Definition**: The maximum amount of credit that a financial institution extends to a client through a line of credit as well as the maximum amount a credit card company allows a borrower to spend on a single card.
- **Usage**: Use "Credit Limit" consistently. Avoid terms like "Max limit" or "Borrowing cap".

### Utilization
- **Definition**: The percentage of a borrower's total available credit that is currently being used.
- **Usage**: Typically shown as a percentage. Low utilization (under 30%) is generally considered good.

### Default
- **Definition**: Failure to repay a debt including interest or principal on a loan or security.
- **Usage**: Use carefully as it implies severe consequences. Often indicated with danger or error color tokens.

### Dutch Auction
- **Definition**: A public offering auction structure in which the price of the offering is set after taking in all bids to determine the highest price at which the total offering can be sold.
- **Usage**: Use exactly as "Dutch Auction".

## UI Copy Guidelines
- **Be direct**: Use clear, actionable language.
- **Avoid jargon**: Where technical terms must be used, provide an `InlineTermTooltip`.
- **Consistency**: Stick to the terms defined in this guide.
